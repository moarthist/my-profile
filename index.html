About Me

I have a diverse academic background that spans translation, art history, and visual studies, with a strong focus on research and cultural heritage. After earning my Bachelor's degree in English Translation, I pursued a Master's in Islamic Art History, where I conducted an in-depth transtextual study on Kalamkari textiles of India and Iran. Currently, I am furthering my expertise through a Master's program in Visual Arts, Museology, and Curatorship at the University of Bologna, with a particular focus on virtual exhibitions and museums. My studies explore the intersection of digital technology and cultural heritage, examining how virtual spaces can enhance accessibility, preservation, and engagement with art and historical artifacts. Through my research and academic work, I aim to contribute to the evolving landscape of museum curation and digital storytelling.
